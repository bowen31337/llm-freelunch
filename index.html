The "Reverse Reasoning" Breakthrough

Most LLMs use "Chain-of-Thought" (CoT) to think forward from the problem. Verification-First (VF) forces the model to think backward from a potential answer.

Verification-First (VF)

verified
Prompt: "A possible answer is 1. Verify it first..."

Verification Step: "If answer is 1... Bat is 2. Total 3. Incorrect."

Insight: "Wait, checking 1 forced me to model the equation correctly."

check_circle Result: Solves correctly ($0.05).

lightbulb Why "Random" Answers Work

The paper leverages two psychological principles: "Check" phase of problem solving (Polya, 1957) and Overcoming Egocentrism (Piaget, 1976).

When an LLM generates an answer from scratch, it commits to its own "intuition" (often hallucinating). When asked to critique an external answer (even a dummy one like "1"), it enters a "Critic Mode", using critical thinking skills that are usually dormant.
